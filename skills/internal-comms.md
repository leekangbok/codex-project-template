# Internal Communications

This skill provides resources for writing effective internal communications.

## When to Use

Use this skill when asked to write:
- Status reports
- Leadership updates
- 3P (Personal) updates
- Company newsletters
- Project updates
- Incident reports
- FAQs or wiki pages
- Any other internal corporate communications

## General Principles

- **Brevity**: Respect people's time. Get to the point quickly.
- **Clarity**: Use simple language. Avoid jargon unless the audience is technical.
- **Action-Oriented**: Clearly state what people need to do.
- **Visual Hierarchy**: Use formatting (bolding, lists, headers) to make content scannable.

## Common Formats

### Status Reports / Updates (3Ps)

A standard format for recurring individual or project updates:

- **Progress**: What was accomplished since the last update.
- **Plans**: What is planned for the next period.
- **Problems (or Priorities)**: Blockers, risks, or key things needing attention.

### Incident Reports (Post-Mortems)

Use this structure for communicating after a significant issue:

1.  **Executive Summary**: High-level what happened and impact.
2.  **Timeline**: Key events and detection/resolution times.
3.  **Root Cause**: Why it happened (without blame).
4.  **Action Items**: Specific steps to prevent recurrence.

### Leadership Updates

- **The TL;DR**: One-sentence summary.
- **The "So What?"**: Why this matters to the business or organization.
- **The Ask**: What do you need from leadership (approval, budget, visibility)?
- **Details**: Brief supporting context.

## Style Guidelines

- **Active Voice**: Use "We shipped X" instead of "X was shipped."
- **Formatting**: Use bold text for key takeaways in long paragraphs. Use bullet points for lists.
- **Tone**: Professional, transparent, and appropriately informal/formal based on company culture.
- **Links**: Always provide links to relevant documents, tickets, or dashboards for those who want to dive deeper.
